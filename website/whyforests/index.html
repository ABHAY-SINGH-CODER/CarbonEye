Why Forests Matter

Understanding the critical role forests play in our planet's ecosystem

31% of Earth's land covered by forests
10M hectares lost annually
80% of terrestrial biodiversity

Climate Regulation

Forests act as carbon sinks, absorbing CO₂ from the atmosphere and storing it in trees, soil, and vegetation. They regulate local and global climate patterns, influencing precipitation and temperature.

Biodiversity Protection

Forests are home to 80% of terrestrial biodiversity. They provide habitat for countless species and maintain ecological balance through complex interconnected ecosystems.

Human Livelihoods

Over 1.6 billion people depend on forests for their livelihoods. Forests provide timber, medicine, food, and clean water while supporting indigenous communities and rural economies.

The Urgency

With deforestation rates accelerating due to agriculture, logging, and urban expansion, monitoring forest health through satellite imaging has become critical for conservation efforts and climate action.
